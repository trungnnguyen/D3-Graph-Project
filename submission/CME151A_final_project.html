Graph Data Visualization


By Vishal Subbiah and Alex Infanger


Hey. Here's a D3.js graph tool to help scientists and mathematicians quickly see the graphs of adjacency and incident matrices that arise during their work. You can input your matrix data in dense, CSR, CSC, and COO format. For CSR (CSC), we expect three rows of comma separated values for the: value (value), column index (row index), and row-start-index (column-start-index). For COO we expect the data to come in row-major sorted form with three rows of comma separated values for the: value, row index, and column index. Edge weights turn into spring forces between nodes and we input some gravity to keep everything centered on the and negative charge to make sure the graph is well-spaced. Upload your file below or scroll down to see some graphs. The textbox allows you to modify the strength between nodes (from 0 to 100). This tool is based in part on Mike Bonstock's Force Directed Graph visualization .
